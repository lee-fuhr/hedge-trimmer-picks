Hedge trimmer questions answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

Cordless hedge trimmers offer the convenience of gas power without the hassle of cords, but battery life and blade length are key factors. For most home landscapes, a 20- to 24-inch blade with a good battery system is the sweet spot. Focus on voltage, blade gap, and weight for comfort.

How long does a cordless hedge trimmer battery last?

Runtime depends heavily on the battery voltage and amp-hour rating. Most homeowner-grade models with a 20V to 24V battery will run for 30 to 45 minutes of continuous trimming. Higher-voltage platforms like 40V or 56V can push past an hour, especially with larger-capacity batteries. Keep in mind that thick, dense growth drains the battery faster. If you have a long hedge or multiple shrubs, buying a second battery or stepping up to a higher-volt system makes a real difference. Many well-known brands like Ryobi, DeWalt, and EGO sell extra batteries, but they add cost and weight.

What blade length do I need?

Blade length determines both reach and how much of the hedge you can cut in one pass. For small shrubs and shaping work, an 18- to 20-inch blade is nimble and easy to handle. If you’re maintaining medium to large hedges, a 22- to 24-inch blade is the most common and versatile choice. Longer blades (26 inches and up) are great for tall, sprawling hedges but feel heavier and can be harder to control for detailed cuts. A good rule: match blade length to the width of your typical hedge. Avoid going too long if you have limited upper-body strength. Also pay attention to the blade gap, the space between the teeth. A wider gap (about 3/4 inch) handles thicker stems, while a narrower gap (5/8 inch or less) gives a finer, cleaner cut on light growth.

Are cordless trimmers as powerful as gas?

Not exactly, but for the vast majority of home hedges, they are plenty powerful. Gas trimmers have more torque for thick, woody branches and can run all day on a tank, but they are louder, heavier, and require engine maintenance. A cordless electric trimmer with a 40V or higher system will slice through stems up to about 1/2 inch thick with ease. If you regularly cut very old, overgrown hedges with branches as thick as your thumb, a gas model might be the better tool. For routine shaping and annual pruning, a quality cordless trimmer is quieter, starts instantly, and won’t leave your arm vibrating at the end of the day. Check the trimmer’s maximum cutting capacity (usually listed in the specs) and compare to the thickest growth you expect to tackle.

What voltage should I get?

Voltage is a rough indicator of power, but it’s not the whole story. For light trimming of small bushes and boxwoods, a 20V to 24V trimmer is lightweight and budget-friendly. For thicker hedges and longer runtime, step up to a 40V to 56V platform. These give you the muscle to cut through heavier branches and a longer work window. Higher voltage usually means a bigger, heavier battery, so balance power with comfort. If you already own a battery system from a tool brand like Dewalt, Makita, or Ryobi, consider sticking with that platform, it saves money and simplifies charging. Many homeowners find a 40V system is the sweet spot between performance and weight.

How to maintain a cordless hedge trimmer

Clean the blades after each use. Sap and plant residue build up quickly and cause the trimmer to drag or bind. Wipe them down with a cloth and a mild solvent like rubbing alcohol. Lubricate the blade edge with a light oil (household oil or trimmer-specific lube) before and after each use. Store the trimmer in a dry place with the blade cover on. Batteries should be kept at room temperature and removed from the tool for long-term storage. If the blades get dull (you see frayed cuts or the trimmer struggles), sharpen them with a flat file or replace the blade according to the manual. Don’t forget to check the air vents to keep them clear of debris, overheating can shorten battery life.

Are cordless hedge trimmers worth it?

For most homeowners, yes. Cordless trimmers eliminate the extension cord hassle and the noise and fumes of gas models. You get instant start, variable speed triggers, and often lighter weight, which makes overhead trimming less tiring. The main trade-offs are limited runtime per battery and less brute force for the thickest branches. If you have a large property with miles of hedges, a gas trimmer might still be more practical. But for the typical yard, a cordless electric hedge trimmer from a reputable brand is a reliable, convenient tool that reduces fatigue and keeps maintenance low.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use a hedge trimmer to cut thick branches?

Not really, hedge trimmers are designed for soft, thin stems up to about 1/2 inch in diameter. For anything thicker, use loppers or a pruning saw. Forcing a trimmer through a branch thicker than its maximum cutting capacity can damage the blades and motor.

How do I sharpen hedge trimmer blades?

Use a flat diamond file or a specialized hedge-trimmer sharpening tool. Work along the factory bevel angle, filing each tooth in the same direction. A light touch is all you need, you’re restoring the edge, not reshaping it. After sharpening, wipe away debris and re-lubricate the blade.

What is the best brand of cordless hedge trimmer?

It depends on your battery platform and budget. Ryobi and Black+Decker offer solid value for homeowners. For heavier use, Dewalt, Makita, and EGO gear provide more power and longer runtime. Stihl also makes excellent battery trimmers in the pro-sumer range. The best brand is the one that fits your existing tools and the kind of trimming you actually do.

Do cordless hedge trimmers have safety features?

Yes, most require two hands to operate: you must hold the rear trigger and front switch simultaneously. This prevents accidental startup. Many also include a hand guard near the front handle and a blade cover that locks on for storage. Some models have a low-battery indicator and automatic shut-off to protect the battery from deep discharge.

How do I store a cordless hedge trimmer?

Clean and dry the blades, then slide the blade cover into place. Store it in a dry indoor location, hanging or on a shelf where the blade won’t be bumped. Remove the battery for long-term storage and keep it in a cool, dry place between 40°F and 80°F. Never store the trimmer outdoors or in a damp shed.

What is the noise level of cordless trimmers?

Cordless trimmers are noticeably quieter than gas models, often in the 85 to 95 decibel range. That’s still loud enough to cause hearing damage over extended periods, so wearing ear protection is a smart idea. Your neighbors will appreciate the reduction in noise compared to a two-stroke engine.

In shortChoosing a cordless hedge trimmer comes down to matching power, blade length, and battery life to the size of your hedges and your comfort. Stick with a battery platform you already own or plan to invest in, and don’t overshoot on voltage if you only have light trimming. A well-chosen cordless trimmer makes hedge work faster, quieter, and a lot less tiring than gas or corded alternatives.
